About Us

On a global level, the coronavirus pandemic has rendered many of the ways we have been accustomed to finding and developing relationships impossible. Isolation has always been a problem at Wellesley because of the heavily academic culture and the difficulty of scheduling time with friends. For many students coming from underrepresented backgrounds, the ins and outs of networking and building relationships with the community of alumni are bewildering and difficult to navigate. Due to the pandemic, there has been a pressing need for alternative methods of organic social interaction and the building of effective support networks for students.

Winterest aims to be a solution to these problems. It is be a mixture of Tinder and Linkedin, where users are matched on the basis of whatâ€™s important to them - interests, values, personalities, and demographic traits (such as like being a first generation student or of a minority group). Our aim is to create a way for the Wellesley community to create and maintain meaningful relationships in order to intentionally overcome the isolation produced by digital-first communication and continue to provide valuable connections across existing groups going into the future.
